Building on the successes of the first three CSWIM meetings (Shanghai, 2007; Kunming, 2008; Guangzhou, 2009), CSWIM 2010 will take place in Wuhan, China. It will be hosted by School of Management, Huazhong University of Science & Technology, which is a top university in China.

Wuhan is the capital of Hubei province and is recognized as the political, economic, financial, cultural, educational and transportation center of central China. It lies at the east of Jianghan Plain, and the intersection of the middle reaches of the Yangtze and Han River. Arising out of the conglomeration of three boroughs, Wuchang, Hankou, and Hanyang, Wuhan is known as the "thoroughfare of nine provinces".

The purpose of the CSWIM is to create a bridge to promote lively exchanges between scholars in China and other countries in the area of information systems and management. In particular, CSWIM focuses on creating a unique experience for IS researchers around the world who would like to communicate and collaborate with China-based scholars, and to study how information systems and technologies affect individuals, businesses, organizations, and societies. In the past three years, about 100 participants attended each workshop, representing most of the top IS programs in North America, China, and Asia.

We invite submissions of original research papers addressing issues concerning the theory, design, development, evaluation, and application of information systems and management. As a research workshop, we encourage submissions of research-in-progress that are innovative and thought-provoking. Research articles particularly sought after are those driven by business problems and validated with proper research methodologies. For a list of topics of interest and submission instructions, see Call for Papers (PDF).
